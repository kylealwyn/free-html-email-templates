Zero One Infinity READMEs


The story of one bug’s surprising lesson


Recently, a user filed a bug — so far, so good. But, in debugging said bug, npm human Ashley Williams made an important discovery. In Zero One Infinity READMEs , Ashley recounts how digging into how the npm registry handles README s led to a history lesson that will definitely affect the future.

Read more on the blog for specifics on the Zero One Infinity rule, plus a cameo from CouchDB.


npm brings history and mis-history to Full Frontal Conference




Oh, and speaking of Ashley … last week, she had the opportunity to talk about npm and modularity at Full Frontal Conference in Brighton, UK. An attendee Gavyn McKenzie wrote a cool recap of the 2-day conf and covered Ashley’s talk.


What we’re reading: On Quiet Developers


In this piece on Redefining Community Engagement in Software Development , developer Seán Hanson makes the argument for embracing and empowering quiet developers by changing our perception of “community engagement.” A very thought-provoking piece for hiring managers and anyone else in an outreach role.

Eve: A human-first programming platform


The creators of the programming language Eve wondered if it could be possible to create a prose first, then code approach to programming. Hailed as a “bold effort to rethink how we program,” Eve has proven successful so far, and it’s quickly gaining popularity in the community. Check Eve out!
